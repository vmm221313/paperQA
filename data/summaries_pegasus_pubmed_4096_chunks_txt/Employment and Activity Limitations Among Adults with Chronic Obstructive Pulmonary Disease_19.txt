<s> the global spread of highly pathogenic avian influenza a ( h5n1 ) virus has reached epidemic proportions in many parts of the world, including the united states.<n> h5n1, which is transmitted by the s. avian influenza virus ( sav ), is responsible for an estimated 2.5 million cases of disease per year, mostly in children and young adults.<n> the high incidence of h5n1 disease in the united states and other countries, coupled with the fact that most of the world population is allergic to h5n1, has become a major public health concern.<n> this review describes the epidemiology of h5n1 in the united states, and the current state of vaccination against it.<n> the main emphasis is on vaccination against h5n1 in children and young adults.</s>
